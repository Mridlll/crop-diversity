How to Read the Indices

Index Intuition Range
Shannon (H′) How surprised would you be picking a random hectare? Higher = more unpredictable = more diverse. Sensitive to rare crops — even a small plot of millets raises it. 0 → ~2.5
Simpson (1−D) Probability that two random hectares grow different crops. 0.9 means 90% chance they differ. Weighted toward dominant crops — less affected by rare ones. 0 → 1
Crop Richness Simply: how many distinct crops are grown? Doesn’t care about evenness — 20 crops where one takes 95% of area scores the same as 20 evenly split crops. 1 → 50+
ABI Composite of all three above, each normalized to 0–1 and averaged. Gives a single “diversity score” that balances richness, evenness, and dominance. 0 → 1
Kcal/ha Mean annual caloric output per hectare of cropped land. Computed from crop production × IFCT 2017 energy values. Coconut-dominant districts (Kerala, TN) show very high values due to coconut’s caloric density. Districts with <10k ha cropped area are flagged as unreliable. ~3,000 → 20M

Why these three? Shannon captures information entropy (rare crops matter), Simpson captures dominance probability (common crops matter), and Richness is the raw count. No single index tells the full story — ABI combines them. The weak negative correlation between ABI and Kcal/ha (ρ = −0.18) suggests the diversity–productivity tradeoff is overstated.
